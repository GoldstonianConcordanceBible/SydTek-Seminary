## Purpose

This document establishes the governance structure for SydTek Seminary.

SydTek Seminary exists as the theological education, ministry formation, ordination, and credentialing division within the broader SydTek University and SydTek Scholars ecosystem.

Its governance framework is designed to support:

- Biblical fidelity
- Theological clarity
- Academic accountability
- Ministry formation
- Credential integrity
- Portfolio-based assessment
- Digital registry verification

---

## Governance Principle

SydTek Seminary is governed by the principle:

**Ordination recognizes demonstrated ministry.**

The Seminary does not treat ministry as a title-first system.

Ministry must be demonstrated through:

- Scripture-centered teaching
- Christian character
- Service
- Discipleship
- Theological study
- Public witness
- Ministry portfolio evidence

## Governance Bodies

### Seminary Board

The Seminary Board provides oversight for doctrine, ordination, credentialing, academic standards, and registry integrity.

Responsibilities include:

- Reviewing ordination candidates
- Approving credential standards
- Maintaining the Statement of Faith
- Reviewing curriculum frameworks
- Approving ministry formation tracks
- Maintaining registry policies
- Protecting institutional consistency

---

### Academic Review Committee

The Academic Review Committee oversees theological coursework, curriculum quality, assessment standards, and program development.

Responsibilities include:

- Reviewing course content
- Aligning programs with learning outcomes
- Maintaining curriculum maps
- Reviewing certificate and degree pathways
- Supporting portfolio-based assessment

---

### Ordination Review Committee

The Ordination Review Committee evaluates candidates for ministry recognition.

Responsibilities include:

- Reviewing candidate portfolios
- Reviewing statements of faith
- Reviewing ministry philosophy statements
- Confirming theological competency
- Recommending candidates for licensing or ordination

---

### Digital Credential Committee

The Digital Credential Committee oversees digital badges, certificates, registry identifiers, and optional on-chain credentials.

Responsibilities include:

- Maintaining credential verification standards
- Issuing digital credential guidelines
- Reviewing registry accuracy
- Supporting credential transparency
- Preventing credential misuse

## Credential Authority

SydTek Seminary may issue:

- Certificates of completion
- Ministry formation certificates
- Licensed minister credentials
- Ordained minister credentials
- Senior minister recognition
- Digital badges
- Registry verification identifiers
- Optional on-chain credentials

All credentials must be tied to documented evidence.

---

## Portfolio-Based Review

SydTek Seminary prioritizes portfolio-based evidence.

Acceptable evidence may include:

- Courses completed
- Sermons
- Bible studies
- Books
- Articles
- Research
- Public teaching
- Ministry service
- Community service
- Digital ministry
- Leadership experience
