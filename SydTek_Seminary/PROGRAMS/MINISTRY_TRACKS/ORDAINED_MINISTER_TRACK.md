# Ordained Minister Track

**Program Code:** OMT-600  
**Track Type:** Ordination Pathway  
**Institution:** SydTek Seminary  
**Status:** Draft

---

# Purpose

The Ordained Minister Track provides a formal pathway for candidates seeking full ministry ordination through SydTek Seminary.

Ordination recognizes demonstrated ministry, theological competency, Christian character, public witness, and faithful service.

---

# Core Principle

**Ordination recognizes ministry already being lived.**

SydTek Seminary does not treat ordination as a title-first credential.

Ordination must be supported by:

- Doctrine
- Service
- Teaching
- Discipleship
- Character
- Portfolio evidence

---

# Eligibility Requirements

Candidates should demonstrate:

- Agreement with the SydTek Seminary Statement of Faith
- Completion of foundational theological preparation
- Active ministry service
- Approved ministry portfolio
- Christian character
- Commitment to ongoing discipleship

---

# Recommended Preparation

Candidates are encouraged to complete:

- Certificate in Biblical Studies
- Certificate in Ministry Formation
- Certificate in Digital Ministry
- Certificate in Ethiopian Canon Studies
- Licensed Minister Track

---

# Required Portfolio Evidence

Portfolio evidence may include:

- Sermons
- Bible studies
- Public teaching
- Published books
- Articles
- Research
- Ministry projects
- Community service
- Digital ministry
- Leadership experience

---

# Ordination Review Requirements

Candidates must submit:

## Statement of Faith

A written affirmation of core theological beliefs.

## Personal Testimony

A written account of faith journey, calling, and ministry formation.

## Ministry Philosophy

A written explanation of how the candidate approaches ministry, discipleship, teaching, and service.

## Ministry Portfolio

Documented evidence of active ministry.

## Character Review

Evidence of integrity, accountability, humility, and service.

---

# Review Process

## Step 1 — Application

Candidate submits ordination application.

## Step 2 — Portfolio Submission

Candidate submits documented ministry evidence.

## Step 3 — Doctrine Review

Candidate demonstrates theological competency.

## Step 4 — Ministry Review

Candidate demonstrates active service and teaching ability.

## Step 5 — Committee Recommendation

Ordination Review Committee makes recommendation.

## Step 6 — Seminary Board Approval

Seminary Board grants final approval.

## Step 7 — Commissioning

Candidate receives formal ordination recognition.

---

# Credential Awarded

Approved candidates may receive:

- Ordained Minister Credential
- Registry Listing
- Digital Certificate

- Credential Verification ID
- Optional On-Chain Credential
